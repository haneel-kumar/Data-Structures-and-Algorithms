Complexity & Key Insight
O(N) time
O(K) space — at most K+1 entries in HashMap
Why a HashMap? Unlike the previous problems where we tracked a numeric sum, here we need to know which characters are in the window and how many times each appears. The HashMap lets us add/remove characters in O(1) and instantly check the distinct count via Object.keys(charFrequency).length. When a character's frequency drops to 0 we delete it from the map — that's what keeps the distinct count accurate.
Why still O(N)? The outer for-loop moves windowEnd forward N times. The inner while-loop moves windowStart forward — but only as far as windowEnd has already moved. Each character is added once and removed at most once, so total iterations across both loops ≤ 2N → O(N).
